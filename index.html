Our Story

Built on What We Have Seen

I am Ronnel Jones, and I am building Phoenix Pride Project because I have seen what happens when young adults are rejected for who they are. I have watched the gaps between "services that exist" and "services that actually help" swallow whole futures.

Las Vegas is a national hotspot for trafficking. Hundreds of young adults are identified as victims here every year. LGBTQ+ young adults face unique risks due to family rejection, homelessness, and discrimination. These factors increase susceptibility to exploitation and trafficking. And too many of the shelters, clinics, and programs meant to help are not safe for queer and trans young adults.

I am not a clinician. I am not a politician. I am a person who believes that connection is the first form of survival. Phoenix Pride Project starts there.

Our Values

What We Stand For

Empowerment

We believe in the strength and resilience of LGBTQ+ young adult survivors. Our programs foster self-advocacy, leadership, and personal growth.

Inclusivity & Affirmation

We create safe, welcoming spaces for all, regardless of sexual orientation, gender identity, race, or background. Every person is seen, respected, and valued.

Survivor-Centered Care

The voices, experiences, and needs of survivors guide every aspect of our work. We honor autonomy and dignity above all.

Community & Collaboration

We partner with survivors, peer mentors, local nonprofits, and advocates to strengthen networks and expand access to resources.

Equity & Justice

We address systemic barriers and discrimination that disproportionately impact LGBTQ+ young adults. Our advocacy challenges root causes.

Integrity & Accountability

We operate with transparency and ethical stewardship. We hold ourselves accountable to the communities we serve.

Healing & Hope

We believe healing is possible. Through trauma-informed care and unwavering support, we build pathways to recovery and a brighter future.

Active Now

The Phoenix Navigation Network

This is our starting point. No building. No full-time staff. Just trained volunteers and real connections.

Peer Navigator Program

Five volunteer navigators trained in trauma-informed care, active listening, and resource knowledge. Background checks required. Monthly supervision with the Executive Director.

Know Your Rights Workshops

Virtual format, co-hosted with local legal partners. Quarterly. Topics include housing discrimination, police interaction, foster care rights, and workplace protections. Recorded for later access.

Digital Resource Hub

A curated referral directory on our website. Simple intake form. Categorized resources: housing, legal, health, food, crisis. Mobile-friendly.

Community Pride Circles

Monthly gatherings featuring survivor speakers with paid honoraria and full consent protocols. Suggested donation $25 to $100. Builds our monthly donor base.

Volunteer

Be a Peer Navigator

We are recruiting our first five volunteer peer navigators. This is not a casual volunteer role. It requires a 4-hour trauma-informed training, a background check ($50 to $75, covered by us), and a commitment to monthly supervision.

What You Will Do

  • › Connect LGBTQ+ young adult survivors to housing, legal, health, and crisis resources
  • › Maintain boundaries and confidentiality
  • › Document interactions for outcome tracking
  • › Attend monthly check-ins with the Executive Director

Who We Are Looking For

  • › Survivors, allies, social work students, retired professionals
  • › People who can listen without fixing
  • › People who understand that trust is built slowly
